Makes Wi-Fi context rules work without Location, and explains how when they can't.

Android normally only reveals the connected Wi-Fi network name (SSID) when Location
permission is granted and Location services are switched on. If you'd rather keep Location
off, the app can now read the name directly through Shizuku, root, or a one-time ADB grant
of the DUMP permission ("adb shell pm grant <package> android.permission.DUMP").

When you tap "Use current SSID" and the name can't be read without Location, the app now
shows a short dialog explaining these options (with a copy button for the ADB command)
instead of a dead-end message.

Under the hood, the whole interface is now fully translatable — every on-screen text is
ready for translation. A Language picker has been added (Misc screen); English is the only
option for now. Human translations are very welcome — see CONTRIBUTING.md.
